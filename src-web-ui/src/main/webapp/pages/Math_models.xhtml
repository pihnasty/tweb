Increasing the productivity of coal mining raises the requirements for all technological processes of the mining enterprise. One of the most urgent tasks is to ensure the uninterrupted operation of the underground system, including the conveyor. Modern underground conveyor lines are characterized by considerable length and branching of transport highways, which over time change their parameters: length, topology, etc. For mine conveyor lines, the support at the entrance of the control object (the discrete nature of the load traffic and their variable intensity in the periods of continuous revenues), the internal divisions of the control object (emergency and emergency arresters of the line conveyors), perturbations at the output of the control object, connected with the limited carrying capacity of related transport links (receiving bunker, rail transport, cargo lift) or violation of their normal mode of operation.

Algorithm

The transport delay T, is the magnitude of the variable, which depends on the velocity vuk. Knowledge of this value is necessary for determining the output flow of Quk conveyor. The method of determining this value for the conditions of the variable speed conveyor consists in numerical integration and control of the distance passing through the cargo. A block diagram of an algorithm that implements the procedure for finding the value of the transport delay T and the velocity value vuk (t-T) according to the method.
In block 2, the current value of the conveyor speed Vn is input. The resulting speed value is used to increase the current value of the path traveled by the cargo fragment (block 3). In addition, the value of the integration step increases the current value of the transport delay (block 4). The path passed by each of the fragments of the cargo on the tape can not exceed the length of the conveyor. This condition is controlled in block 5. While the condition is not fulfilled, cyclic calculations are made, which include reducing the distance traveled by deducting from it the intervals corresponding to the moment of arrival of the fragment of cargo loaded onto the tape (block 6). Simultaneously with the corresponding number of integration steps, the transport delay (block 7) decreases and the value of the index increases corresponding to the moment when the fragment of the cargo arrives on the tape (block 8).
