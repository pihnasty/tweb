Control system of Kien

The basic units of automated control of the system of belt conveyors are a set of equipment of the remote control: control panel, control panel adapter, communication line adapter; and a set of conveyor control equipment, which includes a conveyor control unit, linear modules, a shaker and siren module, an acoustic sensor and a power supply. One of the potentially effective methods of control is the use of a frequency converter. Frequency converter is a static converting device designed to control and change the speed of asynchronous electric motors and based on the principle of changing the frequency of power supply. Engine management can be based on the use of the frequency converter interface, as well as with other devices for monitoring and control at a distance. With the help of a frequency converter, it is possible to provide automation of the operation of the system of drives, namely start, braking, change in the speed of the electric motor. The advantages of the frequency converter are the automatic start and the possibility of changing the current. Changing the speed of the electric motor will avoid complex transients in electrical networks, ensuring the operation of the equipment in an economical mode.

Control system of Mazurkiewicz

The principle of operation of the system is based on the correct use of a magnetic sensor located under a movable conveyor belt. The strength of the magnetic field increases when the magnet approaches the sensor; and when the magnet passes the sensor, the sensation of the strength of the magnetic field changes. The method of pulse recording, which works in this way, allows you to accurately measure the time between two measuring points. The resulting measurement data is sent to a computer equipped with appropriate software for obtaining and analyzing data and visualizing the results. An original monitoring system developed on this basis can signal in advance about the occurrence of conditions that are usually accompanied by the future. An important element of the computerized measuring system under discussion is its management system. The measurement control system is a system for controlling instantaneous changes in the length of conveyor belt connections, and consists of three components: a microprocessor, RAM, and a modem. The concept of the control system for conveyor belt connections, developed in this study, is sent to the real-time diagnostics of the object with simultaneous signaling and visualization of its condition. This system collects during its work a large number of different types of data that provide information about a diagnosed object.

After receiving information about the event's replacement, its cost, time, etc., you can choose the most critical critical element that brings most of the losses. From a practical point of view, given the reporting policy at the mine, the most important are simple reports that must be prepared monthly by the head of a particular department. They cover, for example, the analysis of malfunctions, its cost, time of an accident, etc. The Diag Manager system allows you to provide such messages through the Troubleshooting module.

Control System of Gorniak-Zimroz

The constant increase in data volumes, the importance of knowledge and the enormous popularity of computer databases stimulate interest in automation of technology, intelligent and fast data processing, aimed at gaining knowledge about objects that are described by the data contained in the database. Geographic Information Systems (GIS) developed for the acquisition, verification, storage, integration, 3-D analysis and visualization of data in space associated with the Earth's surface, in which each element is described by the coordinates X, Y, or possibly Z, occupy a special place in the class of database systems. The graphic base consists of numerous thematic maps, orthophotomaps and numerical terrain models. The specific objects of the graphical database are associated with a descriptive database, the content of which depends on the type and range of information stored in the databases. The concept put forth by the current authors is based on the use of some data from existing systems. Thematic maps, such as diagrams of the technological system, are constantly updated with the help of geodetic surveying of mines. All machines are inventoried (usually in Excel spreadsheets or MSAcces) in simple databases.
